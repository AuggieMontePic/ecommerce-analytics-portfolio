# Project 1 — Brazilian E-Commerce Sales & Customer Analytics

**Tool:** Google Looker Studio  
**Data Warehouse:** Google BigQuery  
**Dataset:** Olist Brazilian E-Commerce (2017–2018)  
**Dashboard:** 3-page interactive report

---

## Project Overview

This project analyzes 96,500+ delivered orders from the Olist Brazilian 
e-commerce marketplace. The analysis covers sales performance trends, 
customer value segmentation, and geographic distribution across Brazil's 
27 states and 4,000+ cities.

---

## Dashboard Pages

### Page 1 — Executive Summary
High-level business performance overview with monthly revenue trends,
month-over-month growth analysis, and geographic revenue distribution.

**Key visuals:**
- Monthly Revenue & Growth Trend (combo chart)
- Revenue by State (map)
- Top 10 States by Revenue (bar chart)

**Key findings:**
- $15.4M total revenue across 96.5K orders
- Peak revenue month: November 2017
- São Paulo accounts for $2.7M — 17.5% of total revenue
- MoM growth stabilizes around 0-20% through 2018 after early volatility

---

### Page 2 — Customer Value Analysis
Customer segmentation based on RFM principles with spending quartile
analysis and value distribution across customer tiers.

**Key visuals:**
- Customer Value Distribution (donut chart)
- Avg Order Value by Segment (bar chart)
- Value Segment Performance (table)

**Key findings:**
- 59.1% of revenue generated by High-Value One-Timers (top 25% of customers)
- High-Value customers average $371 per order vs $42 for Low-Value segment
- All segments show ~287-292 days since last purchase — consistent recency
- Near-zero repeat purchase rate confirms acquisition-dependent business model

---

### Page 3 — Geographic Sales Analysis
City and state level revenue distribution with regional performance
rankings and top city identification within each state.

**Key visuals:**
- Top 20 Cities by Revenue (bar chart)
- Revenue Distribution by State (treemap)
- Top 5 Cities by State (table)

**Key findings:**
- Sales reach 132 cities across all 27 Brazilian states
- Average city revenue: $59,998
- São Paulo city alone generates $2.3M
- Strong geographic concentration in Southeast Brazil (SP, RJ, MG)

## SQL Techniques Demonstrated

- CTEs (Common Table Expressions)
- Window Functions: LAG, ROW_NUMBER, NTILE, DENSE_RANK
- ARRAY_AGG and ARRAY_LENGTH
- Date Functions: FORMAT_DATE, DATE_DIFF, DATE_TRUNC
- RFM Analysis Framework
- Cohort Analysis Methodology
- Multi-table JOINs across 6 relational tables
- Percentage and margin calculations

---

## Key Business Insight

This dataset revealed a near-zero repeat purchase rate across all customer 
cohorts — a common reality in marketplace e-commerce. Rather than forcing 
a retention narrative onto the data, the analysis was reframed around 
customer acquisition quality and value segmentation. This decision reflects 
a core principle of honest data analysis: let the data tell its true story.
